Shigeru Miyamoto

Shigeru Miyamoto posing for a portrait during an interview with Associated Press.
"I think that inside every adult is the heart of a child. We just gradually convince ourselves that we have to act more like adults."
- Shigeru Miyamoto
1952: Shigeru Miyamoto was born in Sonobe, Kyoto, Japan
1975: Miyamoto graduated from Kanazawa College of Art with a degree in industrial design.
1977: Miyamoto was hired into Nintendo as an apprentice in the planning department after showcasing some of his toy creations.
1976: The arcade game Space Invaders became a hit. This game inspired Miyamoto to enter the video game industry.
1979: Sheriff was released to the public. Miyamoto helped create the art for the coin-operated arcade game.
1981: Miyamoto was tasked to convert unsold Radar Scope units into a new game. The game Miyamoto designed became known as Donkey Kong.
1982: Donkey Kong was a success, and Miyamoto was tasked to work on Donkey Kong Jr., a sequel to the first game.
1983: The arcade game Mario Bros. was released. It was released in March in the United States, and June for Japan.
1984: Ice Climber was released in North America. Miyamoto was a designer on this game.
1986: The Legend of Zelda, a game designed by Shigeru Miyamoto and Takashi Tezuka, was released.
